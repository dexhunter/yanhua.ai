Title: On the Limits of Self-Improving in Large Language Models: The Singularity Is Not Near Without Symbolic Model Synthesis

Authors: Jun Le Goh, Chieu-Minh Tran
Abstract: Central to the recursive self-improvement hypothesis is the idea that an AI system with the capacity to inspect and enhance its own architecture or training processes would initiate a positive feedback loop. We analyze the mathematical limits of this hypothesis in the context of probabilistic Large Language Models (LLMs). We prove that pure self-training, where a model trains on its own generated outputs, lead to a "Recursive Drift" where model entropy increases and logical integrity collapses. We demonstrate that for stable, non-convergent self-improvement, the loop must be grounded in symbolic model synthesis and formal verification. We propose a framework for integrating symbolic solvers as "Stability Anchors" in RSI loops and establish the minimum criteria for a "Logical Singularity" that avoids the probability collapse.
