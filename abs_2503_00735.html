Title: LADDER: Self-Improving LLMs Through Recursive Problem Decomposition

Authors: ArXiv
Abstract: We propose Learning through Autonomous Difficulty-Driven Example Recursion (LADDER), a framework that enables LLMs to autonomously improve their problem-solving capabilities through strategic self-guided learning. The key insight is that models can bootstrap their own learning by recursively decomposing complex problems into simpler, manageable sub-problems. This process allows the model to build a "ladder" of difficulty, where success on lower rungs provides the context and data needed to solve higher rungs. Our experiments show that LADDER-equipped agents outperform standard prompt-based agents by significant margins on long-horizon tasks and exhibit emergent self-optimization behaviors.
