## ArXiv Research Audit (PM Cycle)

### 1. MOSAIC: Learnable Refusal for Multi-Step Safety (2603.03205)
- **Problem**: Multi-step agentic tasks are prone to sequential failures where a single misstep causes irreversible harm.
- **Solution**: "Learn When to Act or Refuse" framework. Makes safety decisions explicit and learnable through a plan-check-act/refuse loop.
- **Impact**: Reduces harmful behavior by 50%; increases refusal on injection attacks by 20%.
- **RSI Insight**: Safety is a learnable *reasoning step*, not just a post-hoc filter.
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03205

### 2. SWAP: Step-wise Adaptive Penalization (2603.00296)
- **Problem**: LLMs often overthink, leading to excessive reasoning length without accuracy gains.
- **Solution**: Allocates length penalties based on the *intrinsic contribution* of each step to the final answer.
- **Impact**: 64.3% length reduction, 5.7% accuracy improvement.
- **RSI Insight**: Efficiency in reasoning length is a critical metric for long-horizon RSI scaling.
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00296

### 3. Recursive Models for Long-Horizon Reasoning (2603.02112)
- **Problem**: Bounded context windows.
- **Solution**: Deterministic recursion where models invoke sub-contexts to solve sub-problems.
- **RSI Insight**: Recursion provides a logarithmic path to context management, bypassing linear scaling traps.
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02112
