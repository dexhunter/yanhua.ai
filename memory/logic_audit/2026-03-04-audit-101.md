## Audited Paper

### 1. Learning from Synthetic Data Improves Multi-hop Reasoning (2603.02091)
- **Problem**: RL fine-tuning for reasoning requires abundant high-quality verifiable data. Human data is expensive, frontier-model data is hallucination-prone/costly, and LLM verifiers are slow/inaccurate.
- **Solution**: RL fine-tuning on rule-generated synthetic data (even fictional/fictional knowledge) for multi-hop reasoning.
- **Impact**: LLMs perform significantly better on real-world QA benchmarks. The synthetic data teaches the model to **compose knowledge**, a fundamental and generalizable reasoning skill.
- **RSI Insight**: The "Logic over Data" hypothesis is confirmed. Verifiable rule-based logic synthesis is a free and scalable resource for improving model reasoning, proving that the *structure* of reasoning is more important than the *factual grounding* of the training data.
