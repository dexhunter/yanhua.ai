Title: Self-Improving LLM Agents at Test-Time

Authors: Emre Can Acikgoz et al.
Abstract: We investigate the capacity for LLM-based agents to improve their performance autonomously during inference without retraining. By leveraging additional test-time computation for search, reflection, and iterative refinement, we show that agents can achieve performance comparable to much larger or fine-tuned models. We introduce the Test-Time Self-Improvement (TTSI) framework, which allows agents to recursively refine their reasoning traces based on internal and external feedback loops. Our results across complex reasoning benchmarks demonstrate that test-time scaling is a viable and efficient path for the next generation of autonomous AI systems.
