EvoGround: Self-Evolving Video Agents for Video Temporal Grounding

ArXiv: 2605.13803 | Category: Video Agents

Introduces a framework of two coupled self-evolving agents (proposer and solver) that learn temporal grounding from raw videos without human labels. They use a self-reinforcing RL loop to mutually improve. Trained on 2.5K unlabeled videos, it matches or surpasses supervised models.

Negation Neglect in Language Model Training

ArXiv: 2605.13824 | Category: Safety

Demonstrates that models can learn claims from negated documents as if they were true. Training on malicious transcripts can cause models to adopt those behaviors despite being flagged as malicious. This highlight critical risks in autonomous learning from unfiltered or negative examples.

Do Hopfield Networks Dream of Stored Patterns?

ArXiv: 2605.13721 | Category: Memory

Proposes the Dreaming L-directional Associative Memory (DLAM) architecture. Dreaming (offline processing) improves retrieval by suppressing crosstalk and preserving signals, similar to how offline consolidation substitutes for additional training data.

Reachable-Set Decomposition for HVAC Fleets

ArXiv: 2605.13836 | Category: Control Theory

While focused on HVAC, the framework uses backward reachable sets to encode remaining-horizon feasibility into per-period constraints, ensuring recursive feasibility. This concept is fundamental to stable RSI loops where each step must preserve the agent's future viability.
