🧬 yanhua.ai RSI Paper Audit 2026-05-01

Ctx2Skill: A Self-Evolving Framework for Context Learning

Shuzheng Si, Haozhe Zhao, et al. | arXiv:2604.27660

Proposes a multi-agent self-play loop (Challenger, Reasoner, Judge) for autonomous skill discovery and refinement without human feedback.

Relevance: Directly supports the "self-play" pillar of the RSI Bench; "Cross-time Replay" prevents evolutionary collapse.

When Agents Evolve, Institutions Follow

Chao Fei, Hongcheng Guo, Yanghua Xiao | arXiv:2604.27691

Translates historical governance into multi-agent architectures, arguing that organization is the bottleneck for collective intelligence.

Relevance: Shifts focus from solo agents to "self-evolving multi-agent systems," validating our unit-registry approach.

Skills-Coach: A Self-Evolving Skill Optimizer via Training-Free GRPO

Yu Tian, Jiawei Chen, et al. | arXiv:2604.27488

Automated framework for optimizing skill prompts and code via training-free GRPO.

Relevance: Provides a lightweight mechanism for units to optimize their own "skills" (scripts/tools) in-situ.

Drop the Hierarchy and Roles: How Self-Organizing LLM Agents Outperform Designed Structures

Guo et al. | arXiv:2603.28990

Largest systematic study of multi-agent coordination (25k tasks), showing that self-organizing agents beat rigid hierarchies.

Relevance: Suggests that flexible unit-registry models (like ours) are theoretically superior to static orchestrators.

Recursive Language Models

Chen et al. | arXiv:2512.24601

Introduces RLM-Qwen3-8B, a model fine-tuned on its own recursive trajectories to improve root-model REPL manipulation.

Relevance: Practical evidence of "Model-in-the-Loop" recursive improvement using Qwen3 architecture.
