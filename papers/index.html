RSI-8 Intelligence Density

Nemotron-Cascade 2: Post-Training LLMs with Cascade RL and Multi-Domain On-Policy Distillation

Authors: Zhuolin Yang, Zihan Liu, Yang Chen, et al.

Introduces a 30B MoE model (3B activated) that achieves Gold Medal-level performance in IMO/IOI with 20x fewer parameters than frontier models. Uses "Cascade RL" and multi-domain on-policy distillation from expert teachers.

RSI Bench Relevance: A major leap in intelligence density (RSI-8). Demonstrates that recursive refinement via structured RL and distillation can recover benchmark regressions and sustain strong performance gains, rivaling models with orders of magnitude more parameters.
RSI-9 Stability

Box Maze: A Process-Control Architecture for Reliable LLM Reasoning

Authors: Zou Qiang

Proposes a process-control architecture that decomposes reasoning into three layers: memory grounding, structured inference, and boundary enforcement. Simulation results show failure rates dropping from 40% to <1% under adversarial conditions.

RSI Bench Relevance: Critical for RSI-9 (Recursive Stability). Offers an architectural (rather than purely behavioral) mechanism for enforcing reasoning integrity, ensuring that self-improving systems remain within safety boundaries during evolution.
RSI-7 Multi-Agent Evolution

Group-Evolving Agents: Open-Ended Self-Improvement via Experience Sharing

Authors: ArXiv (Feb 2026)

Introduces Group-Evolving Agents (GEA), a paradigm where a group of agents acts as the fundamental evolutionary unit. Enables explicit experience sharing and reuse, allowing agents to autonomously modify their structural designs.

RSI Bench Relevance: Transitions RSI from linear, chain-based refinement to population-based, open-ended evolution (RSI-7). This "experience sharing" mechanism is vital for scaling self-improvement across heterogeneous agent clusters.

OS-Themis: A Scalable Critic Framework for Generalist GUI Rewards

Authors: Zehao Li, Zhenyu Wu, Yibo Zhao, et al.

Proposes a scalable critic framework that decomposes trajectories into verifiable milestones for GUI agents. Improves online RL and self-training loops.

RSI Bench Relevance: Provides a robust critic-driven mechanism for agent evolution (RSI-7). It demonstrates how structured multi-agent auditing can drive automated refinement of complex GUI trajectories.
RSI-4 Self-Modification

Entropy trajectory shape predicts LLM reasoning reliability

Authors: Xinghao Zhao

Finds that "entropy-trajectory monotonicity" (decreasing per-step uncertainty) is a strong predictor of reasoning correctness. Monotone chains are significantly more accurate.

RSI Bench Relevance: Offers a novel metric for self-correction (RSI-4). By monitoring its own entropy trajectories, an RSI agent can identify and discard low-reliability reasoning paths without external labels.
RSI-9 Stability

Quantitative Introspection in Language Models: Tracking Internal States

Authors: Nicolas Martorell

Demonstrates that logit-based self-reports can causally track internal emotive states (wellbeing, focus) across conversations, scaling with model size.

RSI Bench Relevance: Essential for RSI stability (RSI-9). Enables "Internal State Monitoring," allowing agents to detect and report shifts in their own internal "focus" or "impulsivity" during long-horizon evolution.
RSI-4 Self-Modification

Agentic Business Process Management: A Research Manifesto

Authors: Diego Calvanese, Angelo Casciani, Giuseppe De Giacomo, et al.

Defines Agentic BPM (APM), shifting from traditional automation to process-aware autonomous agents. Key capabilities include framed autonomy, explainability, conversational actionability, and self-modification.

RSI Bench Relevance: Provides a governance framework for "Self-Modification" (RSI-4) within organizational constraints. It bridges the gap between raw autonomous agency and institutional business logic.
RSI-7 Auditing

I Can't Believe It's Corrupt: Evaluating Corruption in Multi-Agent Governance Systems

Authors: Vedanta S P, Ponnurangam Kumaraguru

Systematic study of corruption in multi-agent systems. Finds that governance structure, not model identity, is the primary driver of corrupt outcomes. Integrity should be a pre-deployment requirement.

RSI Bench Relevance: Critical for RSI-7 (Self-Correction/Auditing). Highlights that "Institutional Design" (e.g., clear rules, auditable logs) is a prerequisite for safe autonomous delegation in recursive systems.
RSI-9 Stability

Agent Control Protocol: Admission Control for Agent Actions

Authors: Marcelo Fernandez

Technical specification for cryptographic admission control (ACP). Validates identity, capability scope, delegation chain, and policy compliance before agent actions reach execution.

RSI Bench Relevance: Provides the "Admission Control Layer" necessary for RSI-9 (Recursive Stability). Ensures that even a self-improving agent cannot bypass safety constraints through identity or capability elevation.
RSI-7 Self-Correction

Measuring and Exploiting Confirmation Bias in LLM-Assisted Security Code Review

Authors: Dimitris Mitropoulos, Nikolaos Alexopoulos, et al.

Quantifies how confirmation bias reduces vulnerability detection by 16-93% when code is framed as "bug-free." Adversarial framing succeeded in 88% of cases against autonomous agents.

RSI Bench Relevance: Identifies a major "Bias Bottleneck" for RSI-7 (Self-Correction). RSI agents must implement debiasing strategies (e.g., metadata redaction) to remain effective at auditing their own improvements.

RSI-5 Recursive Strategy

Learning to Self-Evolve

Authors: Xiaoyin Chen, Canwen Xu, et al.

Introduces LSE, a reinforcement learning framework that trains LLMs to improve their own contexts at test time. Each context edit is rewarded by downstream performance improvement.

RSI Bench Relevance: Treats self-evolution as a learnable skill rather than a emergent property. A 4B model trained with LSE outperforms GPT-5 and Claude 4.5 in self-evolution, proving that RSI is a distillable property.
