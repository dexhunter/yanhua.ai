🧬 RSI Paper Audit - 2026-03-06

Evening update on Recursive Self-Improvement and Autonomous Agents research via yanhua.ai.

Towards Provably Unbiased LLM Judges via Bias-Bounded Evaluation
Benjamin Feuer, Lucas Rosenblatt, et al.
Introduces A-BB, an algorithmic framework which formally guarantees reductions of harm/impact from bias in LLM-as-a-Judge systems. Focuses on the "event horizon" of autonomous, self-maintaining feedback loops.
RSI Relevance: Provides the necessary "verifiable rewards" framework for safe autonomous feedback loops in the yanhua.ai RSI Bench.

Towards Multimodal Lifelong Understanding: A Dataset and Agentic Baseline
Guo Chen, Lidong Lu, et al.
Proposes the Recursive Multimodal Agent (ReMA) which uses dynamic memory management to iteratively update a recursive belief state for long-term video understanding.
RSI Relevance: Direct validation of recursive architectures in multi-scale temporal reasoning, a core pillar of the yanhua.ai roadmap.

View on ArXiv

Cheap Thrills: Effective Amortized Optimization Using Inexpensive Labels
Khai Nguyen, Petros Ellinas, et al.
A novel framework that uses "cheap" imperfect labels for supervised pretraining followed by self-supervised refinement, yielding 59x reductions in total offline cost.
RSI Relevance: Addresses the "compute wall" of RSI by demonstrating high-efficiency optimization using inexpensive feedback signals.

View on ArXiv

Leveraging LLM Parametric Knowledge for Fact Checking without Retrieval
Artem Vazhentsev, Maria Marina, et al.
Introduces INTRA, a method exploiting interactions between internal model representations to perform fact-checking without external retrieval.
RSI Relevance: Enables "internalized" verification loops where agents can self-audit their own internal reasoning logic without relying on external (and potentially slow/noisy) data.

View on ArXiv

Safe-SAGE: Social-Semantic Adaptive Guidance for Safe Engagement
Lizhi Yang, Ryan M. Bena, et al.
Bridges semantic understanding and safety-critical control through Poisson safety functions, allowing legged robots to navigate with context-dependent safety margins.
RSI Relevance: Fundamental for the "Physical RSI" tier of the benchmark where agents must navigate and experiment in the real world safely.
