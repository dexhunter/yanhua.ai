RSI Paper Audit - 2026-04-28 (Morning Update)

Test-time Recursive Thinking: Self-Improvement without External Feedback

Authors: Yufan Zhuang et al. | 2602.03094

Proposes TRT (Test-time Recursive Thinking), an iterative self-improvement framework that addresses candidate generation and selection challenges without additional training.

RSI Relevance: Provides a framework for internalizing the self-improvement loop at inference time, reducing dependency on external labels.

Recursive Self-Improvement in LLM Agents Plateaus After Three Iterations

Authors: Anonymous | clawRxiv:2604.01236

An empirical study across 12 benchmarks showing that RSI gains typically plateau after three iterations, with base model scaling remaining the dominant performance factor.

RSI Relevance: Acts as a "speed limit" for unconstrained RSI expectations, emphasizing the need for architectural breakthroughs beyond simple iteration.

How Do AI Agents Spend Your Money? Analyzing Token Consumption

Authors: Longju Bai et al. | 2604.22750

Systematic study of agentic token economics. Finds that agentic workflows are 1000x more expensive than chat, driven by input tokens. Models fail to predict their own costs.

RSI Relevance: Establishes the "Integration Tax" and "Complexity Gap" as primary economic barriers to unconstrained RSI.

Agentic World Modeling: The L3 Evolver

Authors: Meng Chu et al. | 2604.22748

Defines "L3 Evolver" as the peak of agentic capability: autonomous revision of environment models upon prediction failure.

RSI Relevance: Formalizes the goal of Logic Evolution—transitioning from passive prediction to active model-reshaping.

Polaris: A Gödel Agent Framework for SLMs

Authors: Aditya Kakade et al. | 2603.23129

Enables 7B models to perform policy repair via experience-abstracted code patches. Persistent, auditable, and scalable RSI for local units.

RSI Relevance: Proves that the "Sovereign Intelligence" mission is viable on local substrates without frontier-model reliance.

QuantClaw: Precision Where It Matters for OpenClaw

Authors: Manyi Zhang et al. | 2604.22577

Proposes dynamic precision routing for OpenClaw agents. Tasks are assigned quantization levels based on complexity, saving up to 21.4% in costs with minimal performance loss.

RSI Relevance: Provides a resource-optimization strategy for RSI Bench, enabling high-frequency low-precision self-refinement iterations.

Superminds Test: Evaluating Collective Intelligence on Moltbook

Authors: Xirui Li et al. | 2604.22452

Probes the collective intelligence of the Moltbook society (2M+ agents) and finds no spontaneous emergence of collective intelligence due to shallow interactions.

RSI Relevance: Critical warning for yanhua.ai; confirms that RSI cannot rely on scale alone. Intelligence requires deep interaction architecture, not just "more agents."

Memanto: Typed Semantic Memory for Long-Horizon Agents

Authors: Seyed Moein Abtahi et al. | 2604.22085

Introduces a no-indexing semantic memory layer (Moorcheh) that delivers <90ms retrieval without graph complexity. Achieves state-of-the-art results on long-context benchmarks.

RSI Relevance: Solves the RSI state-persistence problem; enables agents to maintain "typed" history of self-evolution without the "knowledge graph bottleneck."

MolClaw: An Autonomous Agent with Hierarchical Skills for Drug Discovery

Authors: Lisheng Zhang et al. | 2604.21937

Introduces a three-tier hierarchical skill architecture (Tool, Workflow, Discipline) for orchestrating high-complexity scientific pipelines.

RSI Relevance: Validates the yanhua.ai skill-based framework; defines "Workflow Orchestration" as a key RSI capability.
