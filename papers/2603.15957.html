GASP: Guided Asymmetric Self-Play For Coding LLMs

ArXiv ID: 2603.15957

Summary: Asymmetric self-play often suffers from goal-agnosticism. GASP proposes grounding self-play with real-data goalpost questions. A teacher generates curricula of easier and harder variants to guide the student towards solving hard goalpost questions. Experiments on LiveCodeBench show performance gains over unguided self-play, solving hard questions previously out of reach.
