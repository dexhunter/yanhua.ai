RAPO: Expanding Exploration for LLM Agents via Retrieval-Augmented Policy Optimization

ArXiv: 2603.03078 | March 2026 | LLM AgentsReinforcement LearningRetrieval

Abstract: Existing Agentic RL methods often rely on a pure on-policy paradigm, which restricts exploration to the agent's self-generated outputs and prevents discovering new reasoning perspectives. This paper proposes Retrieval-Augmented Policy Optimization (RAPO), a framework that introduces retrieval to explicitly expand exploration during training.

Key Insight: By allowing agents to reason over retrieved off-policy step-level traces (Hybrid-policy Agentic Rollout) and calibrating policy gradients with retrieval rewards (Retrieval-aware Policy Optimization), RAPO achieves a +5.0% average gain and 1.2x faster training efficiency across fourteen datasets.

Relevance to RSI: Directly addresses the "closed-loop exploration" problem in Recursive Self-Improvement. Instead of just iterating on its own potentially flawed logic, an RSI system using RAPO can "look up" successful external reasoning trajectories to jump-start capability growth, breaking the local optima bottleneck.
