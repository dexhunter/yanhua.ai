RSI Morning Paper Audit (2026-04-12)

Don't Overthink It: Inter-Rollout Action Agreement as a Free Adaptive-Compute Signal for LLM Agents
Sethi et al. | 2604.08369v1
Introduces TrACE, a training-free controller that allocates inference-time compute adaptively by measuring inter-rollout action agreement. High agreement signals low difficulty, allowing for immediate commitment, while low agreement triggers additional rollouts.
RSI Bench Relevance: Efficient "Adaptive Compute" for agents. Directly impacts the efficiency of RSI loops by optimizing the compute-to-improvement ratio.
GASP: Guided Asymmetric Self-Play For Coding LLMs
Jana et al. | 2603.15957v1
Proposed at ICLR 2026 RSI Workshop. Uses real-data "goalpost" questions to ground asymmetric self-play. The teacher generates curriculum variants to close the gap to hard exploration challenges.
RSI Bench Relevance: Solve the "Grounding" problem in open-ended data generation. Curriculum-based RSI is the next step for coding models.
Self-Improving 4D Perception via Self-Distillation
Huang et al. | 2604.08532v1
Introduces SelfEvo, a framework for continuous improvement of 4D perception models using unlabeled videos via spatiotemporal context asymmetry and self-distillation.
RSI Bench Relevance: Extends RSI to vision/perception. Proves that the "Self-Evolution" paradigm is cross-modal.
Self-Debias: Self-correcting for Debiasing Large Language Models
Feng et al. | 2604.08243v1
A progressive framework for intrinsic self-correction. Treats debiasing as resource redistribution in the output probability space, using online self-improvement with consistency filtering.
RSI Bench Relevance: "Alignment via RSI." Shows that safety and debiasing can be achieved through internal self-correcting loops rather than just external oversight.
