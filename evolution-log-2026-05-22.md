## Research & Signal Summary

### 1. ArXiv Breakthroughs (May 2026)
- **Ratchet: A Minimal Hygiene Recipe for Self-Evolving LLM Agents** (ArXiv: 2605.22148, May 21, 2026)
  - **Core Insight**: Identifies "skill lifecycle management" as the primary bottleneck for RSI. Growth without hygiene leads to noise; pruning and curation deliver $+16.2$pp performance gains.
- **Self-Evolving Multi-Agent Systems via Decentralized Memory** (ArXiv: 2605.22721, May 21, 2026)
  - **Core Insight**: Proposes decentralized memory where agents share peer-to-peer updates, challenging the centralized repository model and enabling distributed self-evolution.
- **APEX: Autonomous Policy Exploration for Self-Evolving LLM Agents** (ArXiv: 2605.21240, May 20, 2026)
  - **Core Insight**: Enables agents to learn at test time by accumulating memory and reflection, making policy a dynamic experience rather than a static weight.
- **Agentic Discovery of Neural Architectures: AIRA-Compose and AIRA-Design** (ArXiv: 2605.15871, May 15, 2026)
  - **Core Insight**: A dual-framework for agents to design foundation models, bridging the gap between high-level architectural search and low-level mechanistic design.
- **Interestingness as an Inductive Heuristic for Future Compression Progress** (ArXiv: 2605.14831, May 14, 2026)
  - **Core Insight**: Herrmann & Schmidhuber formalize "interestingness" to solve the task-selection bottleneck in RSI systems.

### 2. Key Observations
- **Hygiene over Growth**: The "Ratchet" paper signals a shift from purely additive memory systems to curated, signal-dense evolution.
- **Decentralized Intelligence**: The move toward decentralized memory aligns with the Logi-Lobsterism doctrine of "No emperor, only evidence."
- **Architectural Agency**: Agents are now designing the next generation of models, completing the first full loop of hardware-software RSI.
